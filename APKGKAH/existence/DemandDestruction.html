Demand Destruction via Attention Deficit

This is a more serious posting, no photo essay here.

Nations with a colonized past, like India, have depended on export-oriented economies up until the turn of the century. After a decade of liberalization, the Indian middle-class is well on its way to self-reliance in terms of consumption; we are unwilling to keep producing and accept favors from the consumer of last resort: the American middle class.

The implications of this self-reliance is certainly not lost on Americans or Europeans; they spend sleepless nights worrying about the state of energy, material resources and the environment if two billion people in China and India aspire to live middle-class American or European lives within a generation or two. The short summary is that we will run out of planet.

The will of billions of people, no matter how clueless, to live semi-decent lives, is not easy to overthrow. However, some military and monetary approaches are being devised over the last decade, to compensate for the intractability of political approaches to curbing Asian consumption:

  • Grab resources by force, so that money becomes irrelevant.
  • Use international monetary force (IMF!) to remove regional political protection from domestic (Indian) currency, so that there are small, if any, conversion barriers.
  • Print enormous quantities of "hard" currency without the backing of physical resources, and use this debased currency to pay for goods and services.

But this is not the usual left-wing political invective against the above forces. This is about unintended pathways of consequences of such actions.
